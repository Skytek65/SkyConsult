Our Story

Two decades of community, connection, and exceptional coffee in Northern Colorado

A Legacy of Warmth

For over two decades, Loveland Coffee Company has been more than just a coffee shop—it's been a gathering place for the Northern Colorado community. What began as a local haven for coffee lovers has grown into a beloved institution, serving as the backdrop for countless conversations, creative sessions, and morning rituals.

Five years ago, Christine and Dax Diaz took the helm as proud owners, carrying forward a 20-year legacy of warmth, quality, and genuine connection. Under their guidance, the shop continues to honor its roots while brewing new traditions for the next generation of coffee enthusiasts.

Located at 620 East 29th Street in the heart of Loveland, we've created a space that manages to feel cozy without feeling crowded—a place where the fresh scent of brewed coffee welcomes you, whether you're settling in with a book, catching up with friends, or finding focus for your work.

Our Roaster: Café Richesse

From farm to cup, a relationship built on respect, quality, and social responsibility

A Family Heritage

For over 30 years, the Neves family has been producing coffee in the state of Bahia, Brazil. Wanting to showcase Brazilian specialty coffee, they formed Café Richesse in 1993 in Fort Collins, Colorado. What began as an optimistic backyard enterprise has grown into an industry leader serving the northern Front Range.

Direct Relationships

At the heart of the coffee industry lies a relationship between coffee tree and consumer that is often forgotten. At Café Richesse, they bridge that gap because they are the farmers. Any feedback from our customers can be instantaneously relayed to the people who can make a difference on the farms or in the mills.

Sustainable Practices

Low impact farming has always been a priority. Their coffee is grown using drip-irrigation technology developed at Colorado State University, with water from rivers that run through their farms. Large areas of timber have been left untouched to protect wildlife and preserve the microclimate of the region.

Socially Conscious Coffee

On land donated by their farm, a full-service school has been operating since 2005, founded by Socially Conscious Coffee—a non-profit organization focusing on coffee farm laborers and harvesters, directly providing education, healthcare, nutrition, and other programs to harvesters and their families.
